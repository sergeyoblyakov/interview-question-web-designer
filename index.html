Interview Question For A Web Designer

Know the common interview question for a web designer in order to be prepared to slay that conference to get a job! And remember, just be yourself.

Be yourself!

That’s the most common suggestion when you are facing an interview. While that is great advice, you also need to be prepared.

One way to be prepared is to know the common interview question for a web designer. There are a few commonly asked questions during a web designer interview. Knowing them will help you be prepared.

It’s not about memorizing the questions. It’s more about knowing how to answer them.

Know the important interview question for a web designer

The most important question that an interviewer will ask you is about experience. Do you have web design experience? What is your experience in web design?

The questions are along those lines. The answer, of course, is your portfolio.

The best way to answer this is to showcase your portfolio. Years can give you experience, but it’s not the be-all and end-all.

The best experience is the actual design process.

If you are interviewing to be part of a design agency, the best way to showcase experience is to show range.

A website agency is versatile. It is involved in various projects. In your interview, you need to showcase that you are also versatile.

Present a diverse portfolio.

Not only that. During the interview process, you have to make it known that you can do almost anything.

Make sure that you don’t brag though. It’s never a good look to brag.

However, you should be able to show different aspects of web design. Not only that, you should show off projects from different perspectives.

This showcases your ability to be able to work on different projects. Not just work, but to do well on projects.

Versatility also entails adaptability. It means that you can do anything not because you know everything but because you are willing to learn to do everything.

That’s another thing you have to demonstrate during the interview. You need to be able to show that you are willing to learn.

There are so many things to learn during the process of working at an agency.

Did you notice that there is just one question but your answer is already multi-faceted? That’s why it’s important to know what questions are being asked because it helps you to be prepared.

The experience question, though, is a no-brainer.

But there’s more…

Why do you want to be a web designer?

Okay, so maybe this is the first question that will be asked of you. Why do you want to be a web designer?

That’s quite a personal question, which means that each person has a right answer to this. There is also no wrong answer here.

Put a deeper meaning to your answer. Web designers make a lot of money. It’s a very technical job. Not a lot of people can do what they do.

However, saying that you want to be a web designer for the money is just tacky. Saying that it’s the only thing you dreamt about since you were young is also kind of overreaching.

Make your answer meaningful.

Again, touch a lot of facets in your answer. Touch something professional and personal at the same time.

For example, you can say that you love the challenge of making things beautiful as well as helping a startup do well business-wise. Personally, you also love doing a job that allows a lot of growth.

Web design is both of those things.

As a web designer, you help increase conversion for a business. This is particularly important for a startup.

It also follows that you create beautiful things. A website, after all, has to be attractive among other things. The other important component is functionality.

As for personal growth, there is responsibility in creating and designing a website. Responsibility makes a lot of room for personal growth.

Why do you think you’re a good fit for this position?

Make a two-pronged approach to answering this question. First, you have to showcase your good traits as a professional and a person. You have to showcase that you have something to offer the company.

On the other hand, you also need to present that you can work well with other people. While you can work on your own, you should also be a team player.

One way to be a good team player is to be good at communicating. You have to be able to communicate as well as listen to anybody communicating.

Communication is always key to collaboration. In order for the collaboration to succeed, there should also be understanding.

Being able to communicate your good qualities to the interviewer will be important, too. This is one way of showing them that you can communicate.

By the way, communication is not merely talking. It’s about being able to state the message across saying too many words.

Thinking on your feet

One important quality of a web designer is the ability to solve problems. An interviewer might ask you to create a web design on the spot.

Understandably, creating a website is going to take time. Obviously, you will not be asked to do exactly that.

What the interviewer needs is for you to be able to think on your feet.

You can ask for a couple of minutes to think about what is being asked of you. However, you should be able to compose an answer.

That’s why it pays that you already know some possible questions so you can prepare your answer. Although, this is quite hard to prepare for because you won’t know what you would have to create until you are already doing the interview.

At least, you already have an idea that this will be needed out of you.

For someone who creates web designs, this part of the interview will not trip you up. Just believe in yourself and go back to the basics.

How do you design a website? You can talk about the steps in creating a website—your steps. This is also one way to tell the interviewer how you work. Give them a glimpse of the work process.

Remember to ask questions though. Asking questions is always part of the process of web design. In order to be able to design the best website, you need to ask the right questions.

A web designer has to satisfy a number of people. There is a client to satisfy as well as the target audience of the website.

In order to satisfy them, you need to have the information needed to create a good website.
